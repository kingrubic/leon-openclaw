# QA rules

## High-risk wording areas

### 1. “Visa-free” vs ETA requirement
If the homepage alert says a nationality is under a visa-free / fee-free regime **and** still tells them to apply for ETA, never simplify this to “không cần ETA”.

Preferred wording:
- “site official hiện thể hiện cơ chế fee-free / visa-free đặc biệt, nhưng vẫn yêu cầu xin ETA trước chuyến đi”

### 2. Business ETA inconsistency
Official pages may disagree on:
- double entry vs multiple entry
- online availability vs mission / DI&E routing

Never flatten this into one rule unless the site becomes internally consistent.

Preferred wording:
- “quy định Business ETA hiện chưa đồng nhất hoàn toàn giữa các trang official”

### 3. Validity ambiguity
Sri Lanka official pages mix:
- ETA usage window before travel
- stay granted on arrival
- extension possibility

Always separate these concepts in the report.

### 4. On-arrival caution
Do not recommend on-arrival ETA as the default route.
Use wording like:
- “limited on-arrival facility”
- “không nên coi đây là phương án mặc định”

### 5. Fee-page gaps
If the fee page shows `----` or unclear cells:
- do not invent a value
- do not infer availability without caveat
- note explicitly that the official page does not clearly state the amount

## Sheet rules
- Match the India workbook style visually.
- Do not force India workbook logic onto Sri Lanka data.
- Use group rows when pricing is group-based.
- Add notes/exceptions rows for special-country regimes.
- Keep a notes column for overrides and caveats.

## Report rules
- Write for Vietnamese leadership first.
- Use strong headings.
- Put executive summary early.
- Separate confirmed facts from caveats.
- Mention alert-based overrides near the fee section, not buried later.

## Safe claims from the March 2026 run
Generally safe to state:
- ETA categories: Tourist / Business / Transit
- passport 6 months, return ticket, sufficient funds
- Transit ETA free up to 2 days
- fees are non-refundable / non-transferable
- homepage alert currently lists 7 nationalities under a fee-free / visa-free special regime while still requiring ETA application

## Claims to avoid stating flatly
- “7 nước đó không cần ETA”
- “Business ETA luôn multiple entry”
- “Business ETA luôn double entry”
- “Mọi business traveler đều xin online như nhau”
- “On-arrival business fee đã xác nhận chắc chắn” when the fee page still shows dashes
