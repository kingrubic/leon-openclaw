# Australia Visa QA Checklist

Use this file before finalizing any report, table, or summary.

## Red flags

Rewrite any statement that does any of the following:

- treats **up to X months** as a guaranteed duration
- merges **stay period**, **visa validity**, and **entry count**
- treats a stream-specific rule as if it applies to the whole subclass
- states **multiple entry** as guaranteed when the page says it depends on the grant
- states a fixed processing time without a same-day official snapshot
- treats australia.com as a policy authority
- uses Executive Traveller as the sole source of a visa-rule claim
- hides nationality/passport eligibility dependency for ETA or eVisitor
- turns `from AUD X` into `AUD X` when the page did not state a fixed universal fee

## Safe wording patterns

Prefer phrasing like:

- `According to the Australian Department of Home Affairs...`
- `Home Affairs states that...`
- `The page indicates a stay of up to ...`
- `The grant may allow ...`
- `Eligibility depends on passport / nationality criteria on the official page.`
- `Processing times should be checked using the official processing-time guide.`

## Product-specific caution notes

### Visitor visa (subclass 600)

- Handle each stream separately.
- Do not write one universal rule for Tourist, Business Visitor, Frequent Traveller, Sponsored Family, and ADS.
- For Tourist stream outside Australia, preserve wording like `3, 6 or 12 months may be granted` if present.

### ETA (subclass 601)

- Preserve the distinction between no visa charge and the app service charge.
- Keep the `eligible passport` limitation visible.
- Do not promise immediate approval; `in most cases` is not a guarantee.

### eVisitor (subclass 651)

- Keep the passport-country eligibility caveat visible.
- Do not imply it is available to all travelers.

### Business Visitor

- Keep `cannot work or sell goods/services` explicit when relevant.
- Be careful with single-entry vs multiple-entry wording.

## Final pass questions

Before delivering, answer these questions:

1. Could a reader mistake a possible grant outcome for a guaranteed one?
2. Did I separate stream-specific facts clearly enough?
3. Did I mark secondary-source content as secondary?
4. Did I leave any unresolved ambiguity unstated?
5. Would a manager reading this over-trust the wording?

If any answer is yes, revise before sending.
